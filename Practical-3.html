Charles Babbage
(1791 – 1871)
  • Full Name: Charles Babbage
  • Born: 26 December 1791, London, England.
  • Died: 18 October 1871, London, England.
  • Profession: Mathematician, Inventor, Engineer, Philosopher.
  • He is widely known as the “Father of the Computer” for his pioneering work in computing machines.
  • In 1822, he designed the Difference Engine, a machine capable of automatically calculating mathematical tables.
  • He later designed the Analytical Engine, which included features similar to modern computers such as a processor, memory, and input/output system.
  • The Analytical Engine used punched cards for instructions, inspired by the Jacquard loom.
  • Ada Lovelace worked with Babbage and wrote the first algorithm intended for the Analytical Engine, making her the first computer programmer.
  • Babbage was Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge University.
  • He contributed to improvements in railway systems and postal services in Britain.
  • He also helped develop early concepts in cryptography and operational research.
  • Although his machines were not completed during his lifetime due to financial and engineering challenges, his designs were proven correct many years later.
  • In 1991, the Science Museum in London built a working model of the Difference Engine based on his original plans.
  • Today, Charles Babbage is remembered as one of the most important pioneers in the history of computer science.
